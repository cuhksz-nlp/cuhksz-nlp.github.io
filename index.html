The Group

Welcome to the CUHK-SZ Language Computation Group. We are a cross-institute research team focusing on natural language processing (NLP) and its related areas, particularly interested in medical NLP and applications. We are affiliated with the School of Data Science (SDS) and the Shenzhen Research Institute of Big Data (SRIBD).

Research Overview

Representation Learning
Texts are typical unstructured information, learning their representations aims to give them reasonable and informative numerical from so as to further facilitate neural machine learning systems for different tasks. In general, text representation is facing the challenge to appropriately represent multiple granularities of language entries, including characters, words, phrases, sentences, paragraphs, and documents, and therefore encode these entries in a unified semantic space. Our research focuses on the following aspects under this topic:
  • Distributed representation scheme (i.e., embedding), and advanced representation learning methods (e.g., pre-trained models);
  • Knowledge-driven representation models for particular tasks;
  • Domain-specific representation models.
Fundamental Language Processing
Fundamental language processing, also known as the core NLP, normally addresses basic NLP problems such as morphological and syntactic analysis of texts. In general, one requires to have a series NLP tasks to perform a full pipeline for core NLP, including word segmentation, part-of-speech (POS) tagging, named entity recognition (NER), parsing (constituency and dependency), semantic role labeling (SRL), etc., which are all covered by this topic in our research. In detail, our group focuses on the following aspects:
  • Constructing a unified framework for fundamental language processing, including several different tasks;
  • Introducing knowledge to different fundamental tasks and design specific models;
  • Research for applying fundamental language processing techniques onto domain-specific scenarios.
Medical Text Analysis
There is abundant textual information in daily clinical practice and such information is of great importance to facilitate smart healthcare to be carried out in our daily lives. In this respect, medical text analysis assembles different techniques to derive valuable insights from textual data so as to drive forward the application of artificial intelligence in the medical domain. The main challenge in this topic is the lack of annotated data and the huge gap between medical and general domain, as well as medical expert is required for specific understandings in this domain. Given the aforementioned fact, this topic covers the following aspects:
  • Smart annotation on medical texts with necessary task labels;
  • Representing medical knowledge to facilitate medical text understanding and generation;
  • Building medical-specific text understanding models for extracting and mining the underlying medical information.
